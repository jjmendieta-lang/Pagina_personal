About me

I am an economist focused on public policy analysis and public investment evaluation, with particular experience in transport, urban mobility, and infrastructure programs. My work combines rigorous economic analysis with an applied understanding of institutional design and policy implementation in the public sector.

I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Economics from the Pontifical Catholic University of Peru (PUCP), graduating in the top 5% of my cohort. My academic training has been complemented by selective extension programs at the Ministry of Economy and Finance (MEF) and the Central Reserve Bank of Peru (BCRP), which strengthened my preparation in applied economics and policy analysis.

Professionally, I have contributed to the design and evaluation of large-scale public investment programs, including cost–benefit analysis, investment prioritization, and institutional diagnostics at national and subnational levels. I am particularly motivated by work that improves the quality of public spending and translates economic evidence into policies with measurable social impact.
